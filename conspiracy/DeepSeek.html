Flat Earth Theory

Origin: Ancient times, revived in modern era Credibility: ★☆☆☆☆

The belief that the Earth is flat rather than a sphere, and that governments and space agencies are involved in a massive cover-up. Proponents claim photos of a spherical Earth are CGI, space travel is faked, and gravity is an illusion.

Modern flat earth theory gained traction in the 19th century through the work of Samuel Rowbotham and later the Flat Earth Society. Despite overwhelming scientific evidence to the contrary, the movement has seen a resurgence in the internet age.

Alleged Evidence:

  • Horizon always appears flat to human eye
  • Antarctica is actually an ice wall surrounding the disk
  • NASA's budget is allegedly used to maintain the deception

Moon Landing Hoax

Origin: 1970s, Bill Kaysing's book Credibility: ★☆☆☆☆

The claim that the Apollo moon landings between 1969-1972 were faked by NASA and possibly other organizations. Theorists suggest the footage was filmed on Earth, possibly at Area 51 or a TV studio.

This theory gained popularity due to perceived anomalies in photographs and videos, the political context of the Space Race, and the technical limitations of the era. Despite being thoroughly debunked, it remains one of the most enduring conspiracy theories.

Alleged Evidence:

  • Waving flag in vacuum (actually due to inertia)
  • Inconsistent shadows (explained by rough terrain)
  • No stars in photos (camera exposure settings)

Reptilian Elite

Origin: David Icke's theories in 1990s Credibility: ★☆☆☆☆

The belief that shape-shifting reptilian aliens control Earth by taking human form and gaining political power. Prominent figures like the British Royal Family, US Presidents, and celebrities are allegedly reptilians.

David Icke popularized this theory, claiming these beings originate from the constellation Draco and feed on negative human energy. The theory incorporates elements of ancient astronaut theories, New Age beliefs, and anti-establishment sentiment.

Alleged Evidence:

  • Strange eye flashes in videos (video compression artifacts)
  • Ancient myths of serpent beings
  • Alleged "slips" where skin appears scaly (pareidolia)

Chemtrails

Origin: 1990s, US Air Force research paper Credibility: ★☆☆☆☆

The theory that the white trails left by aircraft (contrails) are actually chemical or biological agents deliberately sprayed for undisclosed purposes. Suggested motives include weather modification, population control, or mind control.

This theory ignores the well-documented science of contrail formation but has gained traction due to increased air traffic and unusual weather patterns. Government geoengineering research projects like HAARP fuel suspicions despite being unrelated.

Alleged Evidence:

  • Unusual persistence of some contrails (atmospheric conditions)
  • Alleged finding of barium and aluminum in soil samples
  • Patent records for atmospheric delivery systems

Denver International Airport

Origin: 1990s, airport construction Credibility: ★★☆☆☆

The theory that Denver International Airport (DIA) is much more than an airport - possibly a headquarters for the New World Order, a massive underground bunker, or even a concentration camp. The airport's unusual art, architecture, and construction delays fuel these theories.

With its apocalyptic murals, mysterious dedication capstone, and alleged underground tunnels, DIA has become a focal point for conspiracy theorists. The airport's size (larger than Manhattan) and remote location add to the mystery.

Alleged Evidence:

  • Disturbing apocalyptic murals
  • Dedication capstone mentioning "New World Airport Commission"
  • Alleged underground facilities and delayed construction

Paul is Dead

Origin: 1969, Beatles fans Credibility: ★☆☆☆☆

The theory that Paul McCartney died in 1966 and was replaced by a look-alike named William Campbell. Fans claimed to find clues in album covers, lyrics, and songs played backward.

This theory emerged from the Beatles' experimental phase and their playful inclusion of ambiguous imagery. Despite being thoroughly debunked, it remains a classic example of how fans can find patterns where none exist.

Alleged Evidence:

  • "Turn me on, dead man" when "Revolution 9" is played backward
  • Abbey Road cover showing a funeral procession
  • Lyrical references to death in later albums

THEY DON'T WANT YOU TO KNOW

Every conspiracy theory begins with a simple question: "What if?" What if the official story isn't the whole truth? What if powerful forces are manipulating our perception of reality?

1963: JFK Assassination

The theory that Lee Harvey Oswald did not act alone in assassinating President Kennedy, implicating the CIA, mafia, or even Vice President Johnson. The Zapruder film and Warren Report inconsistencies fuel these theories.

1980: Philadelphia Experiment

Alleged military experiment where the USS Eldridge was rendered invisible and teleported. Said to involve Einstein's unified field theory and resulted in crew members being fused with the ship's structure.

1996: TWA Flight 800

The theory that the passenger jet was actually shot down by a missile, with a government cover-up to hide this fact. Eyewitness accounts of a streak of light fuel these claims despite official findings of a fuel tank explosion.

2001: 9/11 Inside Job

The theory that the US government had advance knowledge of or was complicit in the 9/11 attacks. Claims focus on Building 7's collapse, alleged thermite in the dust, and inconsistencies in the official timeline.

2020: COVID-19 Origins

Theories range from the virus being engineered in a lab (possibly as a bioweapon) to being spread through 5G networks. These theories gained traction due to the pandemic's rapid spread and conflicting early information.
